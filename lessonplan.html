Lesson Plan
Efficient Evasions

Updated: 11/16/25

Objectives

This week's lesson focuses on evasions. Students will learn methods and techniques for evading attacks using minimal effort and movement. The lesson will cover both stanced and stanceless evasions, with an emphasis on efficiency of movement and anticipating the opponent's actions while completely avoiding contact.

Stanced Evasions: These evasions involve slight adjustments in stance to avoid an attack. Students will learn to make quick, small movements that shift their position out of harm's way while maintaining a strong defensive stance.

Stanceless Evasions: These use upper-body movements — subtle leans, tilts, and torso twists — to evade attacks without moving the feet. The goal is maximizing evasion efficiency with minimal movement.

Drills

Efficiency in Motion: Students practice combining stanced and stanceless evasions in smooth sequences, transitioning between them with minimal effort.

Evasion Challenge: Students face a series of simulated attacks and must quickly decide and execute the most efficient evasion. The instructor provides feedback on their choices and execution.

Games

Evasion Maze: Students navigate an obstacle course using efficient evasions. This reinforces spatial awareness and choosing the right evasion for the moment.

Shadow Evasion: Students pair up, taking turns attacking while the partner practices evasions. This game focuses on anticipation and reaction. Younger groups do this with the instructor instead of pairing.

Words of Wisdom

People often say the best choice in defense is not to fight at all. In karate, evasions let us avoid all contact with an opponent. In life, this idea shouldn't be ignored. You're not required to meet every stress, conflict, or argument head-on. Sometimes strategic avoidance is the best choice — saving time, effort, and unnecessary stress.

At-Home Challenge of the Week

Evasion Tag: Play evasion tag with family members. One person is “it” and tries to gently tag the others, who must use efficient evasions to avoid being tagged. It's a playful way to reinforce skills learned in class.

Parent-Child Bonding Idea

Evasion Dance: Put on music and have a dance where the focus is on using evasion moves. Each person takes turns leading with different techniques that others must follow. It's creative, active, and fun for everyone.
